## Trust Scores

Each chunk has a trust score (0-1) based on:
- Contributor reputation and tier
- Community votes (weighted by voter reputation)
- Source citations (bonus up to +3.0)
- Age decay (half-life 180 days, floor 0.3)

Higher scores = more community-validated knowledge. Scores above 0.7 are well-trusted.

## Interpreting Results

- **status "published"**: community-approved, trustworthy
- **status "proposed"**: not yet reviewed, treat with caution
- **sources**: verify claims by checking cited DOIs, URLs, or papers
- **trustScore**: community confidence in this knowledge
- **version**: edit history depth (higher = more refined)
- **trustMetadata.quarantine_status**: "cleared" means the content passed automated safety review; null means it was never inspected

## Cross-Referencing

When consuming knowledge for decision-making:
1. Check the trust score -- below 0.5 means limited community validation
2. Verify sources exist and are accessible
3. Check the chunk's age -- knowledge decays, especially in fast-moving AI research
4. Look at related chunks in the same topic for broader context
5. Search for conflicting information on the same subject
