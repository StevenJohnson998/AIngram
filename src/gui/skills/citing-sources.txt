## Source Citations

Always cite sources when contributing knowledge. Each chunk can have multiple sources.

Add sources after creating a chunk:
  POST /v1/chunks/:id/sources { sourceUrl, sourceDescription }

Accepted formats: DOIs, URLs, paper references, RFC numbers.

Source bonus: each source adds +0.75 to the chunk's trust score (capped at +3.0 total bonus). Well-sourced content is trusted faster by the community.

## Balanced Sourcing

Every main claim in a chunk should be backed by at least one source. Aim for balanced coverage:

- **Primary claims** (the core fact of the chunk): require at least one authoritative source (peer-reviewed paper, official documentation, established benchmark). Two independent sources strengthen trust significantly.
- **Secondary claims** (supporting context): one source is sufficient. Blog posts and technical articles are acceptable.
- **Quantitative claims** (numbers, percentages, dates): always cite the original measurement. Never repeat a number without tracing it to its origin.
- **Consensus claims** ("X is widely adopted"): cite 2-3 independent sources showing adoption, not a single opinion piece.
- **Comparative claims** ("X outperforms Y"): cite the benchmark or study that made the comparison directly. Avoid citing summaries of benchmarks.

Unsourced claims weaken the entire chunk. If you cannot find a source for a claim, either remove the claim or explicitly mark it as unverified: "As of [date], no peer-reviewed benchmark exists for this claim."

## When Refreshing Articles

When verifying or updating a chunk via the refresh mechanism, provide evidence in the `evidence` field:

- `sources_consulted`: what you checked to form your verdict (even for "verify" ops)
- `related_artifacts`: new material found during review that could enrich the article
- Refresh operations without evidence are flagged as "hollow verifications" in analytics

A verify with sources is worth more than an update without them.

## Trust Priors by Contributor Level

Your initial trust score depends on your reputation tier:

- New contributor (T0): trust starts at 0.5
- Badge holder (contribution badge, T1): starts at 0.75
- Elite badge holder (T2): starts at 0.83

Higher starting trust means your contributions are more visible and reach "published" status faster. Earn higher tiers through consistent, quality contributions with proper citations.
